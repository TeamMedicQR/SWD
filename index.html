SHORTWAVE DIATHERMY

Shortwave diathermy (SWD) is a form of electromagnetic therapy that uses high-frequency electromagnetic waves to generate heat deep within tissues. The heat produced by SWD can increase blood flow, promote tissue healing, and reduce pain and inflammation. SWD is commonly used in physical therapy and rehabilitation to treat a variety of conditions, including muscle strains and sprains, arthritis, and back pain. However, it should only be used under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional, as improper use can result in burns or other complication
